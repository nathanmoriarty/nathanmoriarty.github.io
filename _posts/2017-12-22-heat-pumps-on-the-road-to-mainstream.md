Is there a catch ? and what exactly is a heat pump ?</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c1">In a recent survey Ipsos Mori found that 68% of householders had not heard of a heatpump [</span><span class="c1 c10">Ipsos Mori 2013</span><span class="c2 c1">].</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c1 c13">The technical details</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">Heat pumps tap into natural thermal inertia from the surrounding environment and &lsquo;pump&rsquo; warmth into the house.</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">This thermodynamic effect is a bit like a seesaw &ndash; you lean on one side and the other rises. Considering graph 1. At even as low as -10 (Deg C) outside</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">you could have a COP of 20. This means 20 times more heat energy than you pay for in electrical energy !</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c0 c20"><span class="c2 c1"></span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">Realistically a COP between 3-5 is achievable however that&rsquo;s still 5 times more bang for buck.</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c13 c1">So where&rsquo;s the catch ?</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c0"><span class="c2 c1">With a heat pump what we are looking at fundamentally a fridge in reverse.
