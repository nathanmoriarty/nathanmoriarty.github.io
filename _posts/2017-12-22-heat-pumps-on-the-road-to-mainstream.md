class="c18"><p class="c2"><span class="c0">Heat pumps could be our only hope of low carbon heat. There are only a couple of options to achieve this:</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c14">Option A</span><span class="c0">: calls on energy giants to decarbonise gas, however:</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2 c5"><span>1.</span><span class="c4">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="c0">Very strong price signals are needed to transition from natural gas</span></p><p class="c2 c5"><span>2.</span><span class="c4">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="c0">Expertise is required from established companies who profit from status quo.</span></p><p class="c2 c3"><span class="c0"></span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c14">Option B</span><span class="c0">: calls upon us, as prosumers, to install heat pumps, however:</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2 c5"><span>1.</span><span class="c4">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="c0">They are currently more expensive than conventional boilers due to installation</span></p><p class="c2 c5"><span>2.</span><span class="c4">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="c0">Households must be insulated to a high standard, have underfloor heating, and solar panels prior to thinking about one.</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">But are we ready? Is there a catch ? and what exactly is a heat pump ?</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span>In a recent survey Ipsos Mori found that 68% of householders had not heard of a heatpump [</span><span class="c9">Ipsos Mori 2013</span><span class="c0">].</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c16">The technical details</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">Heat pumps tap into natural thermal inertia from the surrounding environment and &lsquo;pump&rsquo; warmth into the house.</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">This thermodynamic effect is a bit like a seesaw &ndash; you lean on one side and the other rises. This is measured with a factor called a COP &ndash; coefficient of performance:</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c14">COP</span><span class="c8">heating</span><span class="c14">&nbsp;= T</span><span class="c8">inside</span><span class="c14">&nbsp;/ (T</span><span class="c8">inside</span><span class="c14">&nbsp;&ndash; T</span><span class="c8">outside</span><span class="c14 c22">)</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">This factor is the exciting part. Considering graph 1. At even as low as -10 (Deg C) outside</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">you could have a COP of 20. This means 20 times more heat energy than you pay for in electrical energy !</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2 c3"><span class="c0"></span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">Realistically a COP between 3-5 is achievable however that&rsquo;s still 5 times more bang for buck.</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c16">So where&rsquo;s the catch ?</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">With a heat pump what we are looking at fundamentally a fridge in reverse. Consider a freezer, the metallic element at the back is dissipating heat from the inside to the outside.</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">Therein lies the issue, you have a big fridge strapped to the outside of your house. This unit must be on the exterior of the property for air exchange.</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c2"><span class="c0">Figure 1 &ndash; Heat Pump
