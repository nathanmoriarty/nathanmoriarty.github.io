&nbsp; The September sun was bright, low in the sky, but still warm. </span></p><p class="c1 c16"><span class="c13">The respite of summer rolls on to the bite of winter. </span></p><p class="c1 c16"><span class="c13">&nbsp; &nbsp; Quiet this evening and season, harvest toil ended, some leases renewed, however many released. </span></p><p class="c1 c16"><span class="c14 c21">Ground stands abound ready for fresh hands.</span></p><p class="c1"><span class="c7">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="c14 c30">Land abound - </span><span class="c3">Nathan Moriarty 2018</span></p><p class="c1 c8"><span class="c3"></span></p><p class="c1 c8"><span class="c3"></span></p><p class="c1"><span class="c0">Allotment Co-Benefits</span></p><p class="c1 c8"><span class="c3"></span></p><p class="c1 c9"><span class="c7">The Energy Group (</span><span class="c10 c7"><a class="c6" href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.transitioncambridge.org/wiki/TTEnergy/HomePage&amp;sa=D&amp;ust=1577396212620000">Cambridge Transition</a></span><span class="c7">) recently discussed eco actions that come with co-benefits. It was resoundingly agreed that gardening, or keeping an allotment, has many co-benefits. Although science is rarely used in a sales pitch, the science is nonetheless there. A meta-analysis elucidated a wide range of benefits that arise from horticulture including reduced anger, anxiety, depression, obesity, fatigue, stress, and tension (</span><span class="c10 c7"><a class="c6" href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2211335516301401&amp;sa=D&amp;ust=1577396212620000">Soga, 2017</a></span><span class="c5">). The study also highlights improved mood, self esteem, life satisfaction, and sense of community. The causal mechanisms are unclear however I personally testify for these remarkable outcomes after taking on an allotment. A patch of land is just a tabula rasa. You make it great. Some use them to get some peace and quiet, others reach out and meet others in the community. I also find that through self sustenance a calm and open mind blooms.</span></p><p class="c1 c8 c9"><span class="c5"></span></p><p class="c1 c9"><span class="c7">One also connects with nature. An amazing array of creatures share the habitat. I believe we should think beyond the economic argument for sustainable living (</span><span class="c10 c7"><a class="c6" href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.ucsusa.org/resources/economic-facts-support-united-states-action-curb-global-warming%23.W782dubTWf0&amp;sa=D&amp;ust=1577396212621000">UCSUSA, 2009</a></span><span class="c5">), and take enjoyment from being custodians of this beautiful world. We share the 100% organic plot with goat moth larvae, mice, shield beetles, and many butterflies amongst a further myriad of creatures. It is nature&rsquo;s stage and the show dances through the seasons altering one&rsquo;s perspective.</span></p><p class="c1 c8"><span class="c11"></span></p><p class="c20"><span style="overflow: hidden; display: inline-block; margin: 0.00px 0.00px; border: 0.00px solid #000000; transform: rotate(0.00rad) translateZ(0px); -webkit-transform: rotate(0.00rad) translateZ(0px); width: 601.70px; height: 450.67px;"><img alt="" src="images/image1.jpg" style="width: 601.70px; height: 450.67px; margin-left: 0.00px; margin-top: 0.00px; transform: rotate(0.00rad) translateZ(0px); -webkit-transform: rotate(0.00rad) translateZ(0px);" title=""></span></p><p class="c20"><span class="c15 c28">Figure 1: Parsnip and Radish Haul</span></p><p class="c1 c25"><span class="c15 c19">We took on this allotment fall 2017 and have enjoyed a year of bounty. <br>The first harvest was perpetual spinach. The joyest harvest was parsnips. The easiest were chillies in the greenhouse.</span></p><p class="c1 c8"><span class="c11"></span></p><p class="c1"><span class="c11">&nbsp;</span></p><p class="c1 c9"><span class="c5">The sustainability benefits are also obvious. One can be careless from a use of CO2 intensive fertilisers, compost or nitrogen. On the whole however there is a potential for reduced freight, food waste, and the burning of fossil fuels. There are also opportunities for recycling through composting, CDs, plastic bottles etc. Again there can be issues from some attempts to recycle, such as the use of tires for raised beds as these degrade and pollute. Reusing suitable things reduces landfill and incineration.</span></p><p class="c1 c8 c9"><span class="c5"></span></p><p class="c1 c9"><span class="c5">The most obvious benefit is of course the food. It comes with no plastic wrap or preservatives. It is tastier, more convenient, and thoroughly satisfying. And it&rsquo;s also not soaked in poisonous pesticides.</span></p><p class="c1 c8 c9"><span class="c5"></span></p><p class="c1 c9"><span class="c7">So you might be thinking &lsquo;So what ? . . . this is not possible for me.&rsquo; Well, since the 1908 Small Holdings and Allotments Act local councils must provide sufficient allotments (</span><span class="c10 c7"><a class="c6" href="https://www.google.com/url?q=http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1908/36/pdfs/ukpga_19080036_en.pdf&amp;sa=D&amp;ust=1577396212622000">.GOV, 1908</a></span><span class="c5">). Further developments through recent history have entrenched the right to access an allotment (NSALG, ). They are for all.</span></p><p class="c1 c8 c9"><span class="c5"></span></p><p class="c1 c9"><span class="c5">So you are sold !
